Plank

Bodyweight • Core Stability

Home Exercises Progress Tracker

Purpose

Builds deep core stability by training the abdominals, glutes, shoulders, and lower back to work together. Essential for protecting the spine and improving performance in squats, deadlifts, and push-ups.

Instructions

  1. Place forearms on the floor directly under your shoulders.
  2. Extend legs behind you, resting on toes.
  3. Keep body in a straight line from head to heels.
  4. Engage core and glutes by gently bracing your abs.
  5. Breathe slowly and hold the position.

Safety Tips

  • Avoid sagging hips or raising them too high.
  • Keep neck neutral—do not look up or down excessively.
  • Stop if you feel lower-back pain.

Progressions

  • Beginner: Knees on the floor.
  • Standard: Full plank on toes.
  • Advanced: Shoulder taps or side planks.
