Push-Ups

Bodyweight Upper Body Exercise

Home Exercises Progress Tracker

Purpose

Builds strength in chest, shoulders, triceps, and core while improving full-body stability and coordination. A foundational body-weight movement.

Instructions

  1. Place hands on the floor slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  2. Extend legs behind you, forming a straight line from head to heels.
  3. Engage your core and glutes.
  4. Lower your chest toward the floor by bending elbows.
  5. Keep elbows at about a 30–45° angle from your body.
  6. Press through your palms to return to the starting position.

Safety Tips

  • Do not let hips sag or pike upward.
  • Keep your neck neutral—don’t look up.
  • Stop the set when form starts breaking down.
  • Use knees or incline if full push-ups are too difficult.

Progressions

  • Wall push-ups
  • Incline push-ups (bench or box)
  • Knee push-ups
  • Standard push-ups
  • Slow tempo or paused push-ups
